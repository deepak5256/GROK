General Information

Lalbagh, located in the heart of Bengaluru, India, is a magnificent botanical garden that encapsulates the city's rich natural beauty and cultural heritage. Spread over 240 acres, Lalbagh is a captivating oasis, offering respite from the bustling urban landscape. Established in the 18th century by Hyder Ali and later expanded by his son Tipu Sultan, this historic garden is a testament to their love for horticulture. As one strolls through its well-manicured pathways, they are greeted by a stunning array of plant species from around the world, including towering ancient trees, vibrant flower beds, and enchanting water bodies. The centerpiece of Lalbagh is the majestic Glass House, reminiscent of London's Crystal Palace, where an annual flower show attracts horticulturists and nature enthusiasts from far and wide. Lalbagh is not only a botanical treasure trove but also a haven for birdwatchers, as it hosts numerous avian species, transforming it into a bird lover's paradise. Beyond its natural splendor, Lalbagh holds historical significance, with monuments like the Lalbagh Rock, which dates back millions of years, and the Kempegowda Tower, a four-story tower built to commemorate the founder of Bengaluru. Whether one seeks solace amidst nature, explores the vibrant flora, or immerses themselves in the rich history, Lalbagh is a sanctuary that continues to captivate and inspire visitors with its timeless charm.

Geological Information

Lalbagh, located in the southern part of Bangalore, India, is a renowned botanical garden that holds significant geological features. The region where Lalbagh is situated lies within the Deccan Plateau, a vast volcanic plateau formed by extensive volcanic activity around 65 million years ago. The garden itself is nestled amidst the ancient Peninsular Gneissic Complex, a rock formation comprising granitic gneisses, charnockites, and migmatites. These rocks provide a glimpse into the complex geological history of the region, showcasing the intense metamorphic processes they have undergone over millions of years. Lalbagh's geological setting also reflects the presence of residual hills and ridges, remnants of the once-extensive rocky landscape. Additionally, the garden houses a serene lake called the 'Lalbagh Lake,' which enhances the scenic beauty of the area and provides a habitat for various aquatic organisms. With its geological heritage and natural charm, Lalbagh stands as a captivating destination that combines the beauty of nature with the fascinating geological legacy of the Deccan Plateau.
