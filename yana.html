General Information

Yana Caves, nestled amidst the lush Western Ghats of Karnataka, India, is a mystical natural wonder that captivates all who visit. Surrounded by dense forests, these ancient limestone formations have stood the test of time, their towering spires reaching towards the sky like sentinels of nature's grandeur. The main attractions, known as Bhairaveshwara Shikhara and Mohini Shikhara, exhibit intricate patterns and unique geological formations that evoke a sense of awe and wonder. As visitors venture into the caves, they are enveloped by an otherworldly atmosphere, as sunlight filters through the canopy above, casting ethereal rays upon the moss-covered rocks. Legends and folklore abound, adding an air of mysticism to the experience. Yana Caves, with their serene beauty and spiritual aura, offer a tranquil escape and a chance to connect with the natural wonders that have existed for centuries.

Geological Information

Yana Caves, located in the state of Karnataka, India, are a fascinating geological marvel that showcases the stunning beauty of nature. These caves are nestled amidst the verdant forests of the Western Ghats, renowned for their rich biodiversity. The Yana Caves consist of two limestone formations known as the Bhairaveshwara Shikhara and the Mohini Shikhara, rising majestically amidst the surrounding lush greenery. These towering rock formations, reaching heights of approximately 90 meters, were created millions of years ago through the process of chemical weathering and erosion. The unique geological features of Yana Caves are enhanced by the presence of natural caves within the formations, which are believed to have been carved out by underground water currents over centuries. These caves serve as a testament to the forces of nature, with their stunning stalactites and stalagmites adorning the interior chambers, creating a mesmerizing spectacle for visitors and geology enthusiasts alike. Yana Caves are not only a geological wonder but also hold great spiritual significance, as they are home to an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, attracting pilgrims and nature lovers from far and wide.
