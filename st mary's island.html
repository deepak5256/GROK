General Information

St. Mary's Island, located off the coast of Karnataka, India, is a mesmerizing natural wonder. Composed of four small islets made entirely of columnar basalt rocks, it offers a breathtaking sight to behold. These unique hexagonal rock formations, shaped by volcanic activity millions of years ago, create a surreal landscape that attracts visitors from all over the world. The islands are surrounded by pristine turquoise waters, adding to their ethereal beauty. Apart from the geological marvels, St. Mary's Island boasts pristine sandy beaches and crystal-clear swimming spots, making it a haven for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts alike. The island's rich biodiversity includes various species of birds, plants, and marine life, making it a treat for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. Accessible by ferry from the mainland, St. Mary's Island offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the sheer beauty and tranquility of this extraordinary destination.

Geological Information

St. Mary's Island, also known as Coconut Island, is a small group of four islands located off the coast of Karnataka in India. Here is some geological information about St. Mary's Island: Formation: St. Mary's Island is a result of volcanic activity that occurred millions of years ago. The basalt rock formations found on the island are believed to have been formed during the Gondwana breakup. Basalt Columns: The island is renowned for its unique basalt rock formations, which have taken the form of hexagonal columns. These columns are a result of the cooling and cracking of lava flows that occurred during volcanic eruptions. Geological Significance: St. Mary's Island is considered a significant geological site in India due to its well-preserved basalt columns. It provides valuable insights into the processes that shape volcanic landscapes and is of great interest to geologists and researchers. Age: The basalt formations on St. Mary's Island are estimated to be around 88 million years old. They are part of the Deccan Traps, a large volcanic province spread across central India. Coastal Erosion: The island's unique geological features have been shaped by coastal erosion over time. The continuous action of waves and tides has sculpted the basalt columns, creating picturesque formations along the shoreline. Geological Heritage Site: St. Mary's Island has been declared a National Geological Monument by the Geological Survey of India. This designation aims to protect and conserve its geological significance and promote scientific understanding and public awareness of its geological heritage. Biodiversity: Apart from its geological significance, St. Mary's Island is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The island supports a variety of plant species, including coconut palms, mangroves, and shrubs. It is also a nesting site for several species of birds. Tourism: Due to its stunning natural beauty and geological significance, St. Mary's Island has become a popular tourist destination. Visitors can explore the unique rock formations, enjoy the pristine beaches, and engage in activities such as swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.
